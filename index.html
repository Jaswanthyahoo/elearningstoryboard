Workshop Training
FEA is a powerful tool – it is used practically in research as well as various engineering applications today – but it must be treated with care and understanding because it is also very easy to get incorrect answers. A saying that is quite common in the industry is “garbage in, garbage out.” FEA will not tell you if you have set up your analysis incorrectly. This means that, even if you understand all of the math and physics at work, simple mistakes in the initial conditions can yield good looking results that are completely wrong. For this reason expert users of FEA will always have a good estimate of the results before they begin. This “sanity check” allows them to ensure that the FEA results are on target. ARTEM workshops are aimed at discussing the theory behind the finite element software’s along with the usage & importance of the tool, unlike the usual seminars/workshops. We are confident the academia/industry will immensely benefit from this unique course.

FAQ's

What is CAE?

Basically, CAE is a method used by engineers to understand how elements in a product would react to different forces being exerted on them. Today, designs are far more complex for an engineer to apply traditional mathematical equations. For instance, how would a building react to different forces being exerted by wind on it, how would parts in an engine behave- will different shapes or thickness have any difference? To solve these, it requires a methodology, i.e., geometric model to replicate the prototype and analyzed with Finite analysis method with the computer Tools.
